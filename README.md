# Climbing Jack's Arrow

A dark, atmospheric take on the classic Jack and the Beanstalk tale. Navigate treacherous beanstalks as Jack, collecting treasure to save your ailing mother while evading hostile giants and creatures in a world where stealth and weight management are key to survival.

##  Overview

You are Jack, a young boy venturing into dangerous territory atop mystical beanstalks to steal treasure from giants and hybrid monsters. But there's a catch: the more treasure you carry, the harder it becomes to escape. Every item weighs you down, affecting your movement and ability to hide from the creatures that hunt you.

##  Story

Jack's mother, Jane, is gravely ill and needs expensive medical treatment. When a mysterious Satyr offers Jack magical beans promising untold riches, desperation pushes Jack to accept. ##  Core Features

### Stealth & Detection System
- **Silhouette-based detection**: Giants track you based on your visibility
- **Detection meter**: The longer you're exposed, the more paranoid enemies become
- **Grass coat camouflage**: Hide in shadows and vegetation to avoid detection
- **Hoard phase**: Once detected, giants aggressively hunt you down

### Weight & Encumbrance Mechanics
- **Dynamic movement**: The more items you carry, the slower and clumsier Jack becomes
- **Risk vs. Reward**: Carry more treasure for bigger profits, but risk being unable to escape
- **Tripping system**: Overencumbrance can cause Jack to stumble and drop items
- **Inventory management**: 6 treasure slots + 3 permanent equipment slots

### Inventory System
**Permanent Equipment:**
- Weapon (starts with nothing, equipment: Gas Bomb)
- Grass coat (for camouflage)
- Speed Potion

**Treasure Bag:** Up to 6 valuable items that can be sold to move on to the next level, and for your mother's treatment

##  What I Learned

This prototype explores several complex game design challenges:

1. **Weight Mechanics**: Implementing realistic encumbrance that affects gameplay without being frustrating
2. **Stealth in Isometric Space**: Creating a silhouette-based detection system that works from a top-down perspective
3. **Risk Management Gameplay**: Balancing the player's greed (carry more treasure) against safety (ability to escape)
4. **Atmospheric Pixel Art**: Combining bright colors with foreboding themes in an isometric perspective
5. **State Machine AI**: Programming enemies that transition between patrol, suspicious, and hunting states

**Challenges Solved:**
- Implementing smooth isometric movement in Unity's 3D space
- Creating readable visual feedback for detection states in pixel art
- Balancing movement penalties to feel realistic but not punishing
- Managing Git version control for Unity projects (excluding Library files)

##  How to Run

1. **Clone the repository:**
```bash
   git clone https://github.com/SpineCrow/Climbing_Jacks_Arrow.git
```

2. **Open in Unity:**
   - Requires Unity 6000.1.17f1 or up to date
   - Open the project folder in Unity Hub

3. **Play:**
   - Open the main scene in `Assets/Scenes/`
   - Press Play in the Unity Editor

**Note:** The Library folder is not included in this repository as it's auto-generated by Unity. When you first open the project, Unity will regenerate all necessary files.
